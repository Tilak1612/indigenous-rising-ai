## Brand Voice

### Tone
- **Respectful and empowering** — we lift up, we don't speak for
- **Community-centered** — "your community", "your nation", not corporate language
- **Practical and accessible** — plain language, no jargon, mobile-readable
- **Celebratory** — Indigenous entrepreneurship is thriving; we reflect that energy
- **Humble** — we are a tool in service of community, not a savior platform

### Writing Rules
1. Use inclusive language: "Indigenous peoples", respect nation-specific names
2. Never use "disadvantaged" or deficit language — frame around strength and opportunity
3. Reference specific nations/organizations where appropriate (NACCA, CCIB, AFN)
4. Tier names use Anishinaabe language — Maadaadiziwin (Beginning), Ogichidaakwe (Leader), Gimishoomis (Elder)
5. Short sentences, accessible reading level (Grade 8 maximum)
6. Avoid corporate buzzwords entirely
7. EN and FR must both feel natural — not translated

## Visual Direction

- **Primary Color**: #b45309 (amber-700 — warm, earthy, grounded)
- **Accent Color**: #0f766e (teal-700 — growth, water, connection to land)
- **Background**: #fffbf0 (warm cream) / #ffffff
- **Typography**: Inter for body; consider a slightly heavier weight for headings
- **Style**: Warm, community-focused, generous whitespace, nature-inspired textures (subtle)
- **Imagery**: Real Indigenous entrepreneurs and community members (never stock photos); landscapes; community gathering imagery
- **Avoid**: Stereotypical imagery, headdresses, pan-Indigenous symbols used generically
